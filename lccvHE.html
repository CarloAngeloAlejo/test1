Home Economics (HE)

SENIOR HIGHSCHOOL DEPARTMENT

A Home Economics program is designed to provide students with practical knowledge and skills related to managing households, nutrition, family and consumer sciences, and various aspects of daily living. This educational curriculum typically covers a range of subjects, including meal planning and preparation, nutrition, textile and clothing care, interior design, child development, family economics, and resource management. Through hands-on experiences and coursework, students learn how to create healthy and balanced meals, manage household budgets, understand child development principles, and develop skills in areas such as sewing and interior decoration. The program aims to empower individuals with the practical tools needed for effective home management and the enhancement of family well-being. Graduates may pursue careers in fields such as nutrition education, family services, or community outreach, or apply their skills in various domestic and family-oriented settings.

Avaiable Programs: Bread and Pastry, Cookery, Food and Beverages, Housekeeping, & Front Office.
